Drip

Bee House

Arguably the simplest and cleanest way to draw out a coffee’s best qualities, the pour-over method is elegant without being prohibitively difficult. For those accustomed to coffee from a drip machine, this method will produce something similar but noticeably more delicate and complex.

What You Need:

  • Bee House Ceramic Brewer
  • #2 Cone Filters
  • Burr Grinder
  • Whole Bean Coffee
  • Goose Neck Kettle
  • Scale
  • Mug

Step #1 Boil Water

The first thing we want to do is fill up our kettle and heat up the water. We want the water to be anywhere from 195℉ - 205℉

Step #2 Fold Filter

Once our water is heated we will want to fold our coffee filter and place it into the dripper.

Step #3 Pre Heat filter

Once our dripper is prepped we want rinse the filter with our hot water to remove any paper filter taste that we might get from the filter.

Step #4 Weigh and Grind Coffee

Once you have weighed your coffee you will want to grind it the size of table salt

Select a Strength:

1:18

1:16

1:14

Step #5 The Bloom

For this step you will want to saturate all your gounds with the lowest amount of water possible. Aim for double the amount of your coffee. Allow your bloom to sit for 30-45 seconds
